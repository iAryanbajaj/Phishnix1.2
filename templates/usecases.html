Notable Phishing Attack Case Studies

Learn from these real-world examples of data breaches caused by phishing attacks

  • Case Study #1: John Podesta's Email Breach

    One of the Biggest Data Breaches from Phishing: John Podesta's Email

    During the November 2016 election, John Podesta, chairman of Hillary Clinton's presidential campaign, became one of the most notable phishing victims. A Russian hacker group known as Fancy Bear sent him an email pretending to be from Google, warning of "unusual log in activity" on his account. The email linked to a malicious website that, when accessed, compromised his Gmail credentials. This led to the eventual release of thousands of Podesta's emails via WikiLeaks in the weeks leading up to the election.

  • Case Study #2: BenefitMall Data Breach

    BenefitMall: HR and Payroll Services Breach

    Between June 2018 and October 2018, BenefitMall, a human resource and payroll administration company, suffered a significant breach when employee email login credentials were compromised through a phishing attack. The exposed information included:

    Names
    Email addresses
    Birth dates
    Bank account information
    Insurance premium payment details

    With a network of more than 20,000 advisors serving over 200,000 businesses, this breach potentially affected a massive number of employees and organizations.

  • Case Study #3: Sony Pictures Hack

    Sony Pictures: Entertainment Industry Devastation

    In November 2014, North Korean government-backed hackers launched a devastating attack on Sony Pictures in retaliation for the film "The Interview." Using phishing and spear-phishing emails containing malware, the attackers gained access to Sony's network and conducted months of covert reconnaissance.

    Once inside, the hackers:

    Threatened company employees and executives
    Stole confidential data
    Disabled thousands of computers

    The attack is estimated to have cost Sony Pictures upwards of $100 million and remains one of the most destructive cyberattacks on a corporation to date.

  • Case Study #4: Methodist Hospitals Breach

    Methodist Hospitals: Healthcare Data Compromised

    In August 2019, Methodist Hospitals in Gary, Indiana, confirmed that a phishing attack had compromised the personal information of more than 68,000 patients. The breach wasn't discovered until June when an employee reported suspicious activity in their email account.

    The investigation revealed that at least two email accounts had been compromised, exposing patient data including:

    Names and addresses
    Health insurance information
    Social Security numbers
    Passport numbers
    Bank account details
    Electronic signatures
    Login credentials
    Dates of birth
    Treatment information

    This case demonstrates how healthcare organizations remain prime targets for phishing attacks due to the sensitive nature of the data they hold.

  • Case Study #5: University of Wisconsin-Parkside

    University of Wisconsin-Parkside: Educational Institution Financial Loss

    In June 2019, the University of Wisconsin-Parkside fell victim to a sophisticated phishing attack that resulted in significant financial loss. An employee received a phishing email prompting them to change the routing numbers for two university system vendors.

    Before the issue was discovered, the university had lost $315,000 through fraudulent bank transfers. This case highlights how even educational institutions are not immune to phishing attacks, and how financial manipulation through phishing can result in direct monetary losses beyond just data breaches.
